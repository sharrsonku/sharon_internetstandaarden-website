Styles

Now you know that graffiti started off with tags. This evolved when they learned about how you can tag in more unique shapes. Later the graffiti street art introduced characters and even more unique styles into the picture. Here you will find 8 of the most used graffiti styles. Hover above the styles and read all about them.

Tags

Tags

Is the easiest and simplest graffiti style there is. It only includes one color and are the most iconic stylised signature. Taggers typically tag their name/nickname.

Stencil

Stencil

Desired forms are cut out of paper and/or cardboard and then applied with spray paint or paint-rollers as such is easy to replicate images and put them up quickly.

Stickers

Stickers

Otherwise known as sticker bombing or slaps. This is a strategy for artist to easily disperse their presence. Artists would print thousands of stickers and slap them onto walls, post and so on.

Blockbuster

Blockbuster

The letters are usually a big block-style. The background is usually painted with paint rollers.

Wildstyle

Wildstyle

Are intricate but visually appealing. Consist of Interlocked letters and other decorative elements. They can have as much colours as you want. Wildstyles are usually difficult to dechiper by someone without graffiti knowledge.

Masterpiece

Masterpiece

Also called a ‘piece’ is painted by freehand and uses at least tree colours. I'ts big, bold and can be as intricate or as simple as the artist wants it to be.

Character

Character

They are usually characterized humans,animals,spray cans and other existing/fantasy creatures or items. Most likely to be painted around or inside a gaffiti.

Throw up/Throwie

Throw up/Throwie

Are rounded letters with two colours. The letters are sprayed first and then the outline.
